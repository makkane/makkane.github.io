I work on Adaptation of Wire Electric Discharge Machining (WEDM) for Slicing Semiconductor Ingots

Abstract: WEDM is an established machining technique which is based on spark-erosion caused by discharge between the tool (a wire) and the workpiece. Previous research at IITB and elsewhere has shown that, WEDM is an effective technique for slicing semiconductor ingots as it is a non-contact method, and it can also save significant material (reduces material loss from 40% to 15%). However, WEDM is not designed for semiconductors. Therefore, there is a need to adapt it for wafer-manufacturing industry. Our research efforts are focussed to address issues in this area.

Here is a short article regarding our research published in IITB ACR Newsletter.
