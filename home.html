    Electromagnetic Compatibility Aspects of Solar Photovoltaics

    Solar energy is clean, but we also need to make sure it is safe electromagnetically. Solar photovoltaic (PV) panels produce DC power. We need power electronic converters (PECs) to (a) optimally utilize the solar panel and (b) to convert the power in customary type of electricity (AC/DC at required voltage/current levels). The PECs have devices which switch at frequencies from tens to hundreds of kHz. Such switching gives rise to signals at high frequencies which might be problematic for (a) grid, (b) other equipment in the vicinity of solar PV.
    The main EMC issues of the following types:
    1. Conducted disturbances to grid: Harmonics (in V and I), voltage unbalance, leakage current
    2. Radiated disturbances: Solar panels installations can act as antennas and cause disturbance to radio frequency operations.
    3. Intra PV system disturbance: The harmonic signals can affect auxiliary equipment for example smart meters, battery chargers, batteries, etc.
    4. PV system as victim: Disturbances from the grid can affect the solar PV system.

    Wire Electric Discharge Machining: Extending its applicability for cutting semiconductors

    WEDM is an established machining technique which is based on spark-erosion caused by discharge between the tool (a wire) and the workpiece. It is already shown that, WEDM is an effective technique for slicing semiconductor ingots because: (a) it is a non-contact method (b) it can also save significant material (reduces material loss from 40% to 15%). However, WEDM is not designed for semiconductors. Therefore, we tried to extend its applicability for semiconductor-slicing. My contributions to this project are as follows:

    1. Characterization of sparks in WEDM of silicon

    When metals are cut with WEDM, the electric discharges take place between two metals. However, when silicon is cut with WEDM, the electric discharge takes palce between a metal and a semiconductor. Using systematic design of experiments, we could show that the metal-semiconductor junction plays an important role in the spark-erosion of silicon.

    2. Design and Control of Pulse Generator for WEDM

    A pulse generator is required to process the material by spark erosion. This is because, if a continuous voltage is applied, it may lead to a short circuit. A 100 V, 10 A, 10 kHz pulse generator was designed for the process and a new pulsed current reference scheme was proposed, which could improve the efficiency of the pulse generator by 30%.

    3. Fabrication of miniature WEDM

    A miniature WEDM (which could fit in a size of 4 ft x 4 ft x 2 ft was developed. It could cut a workpiece of size 10 cm x 10 cm x 10 cm, and it can fit on a table top. It uses water as the dielectric.

    4. Novel electrical supply schemes for Multi-Wire EDM

    Although WEDM has a potential to save a lot of material in manufacturing wafers, it is still not accepted by the industry - one of the main reasons is - 'multi-wire-EDM' is not available which can compete with multi-wire-saw technology. We have tried to remove the hurdle by making multi-wire-EDM more effective. We have proposed novel pulse generator topologies for multi-wire-EDM such that wire vibrations are reduced. This would lead to lesser kerf losses and a possibility to make thinner wafers. Download thesis here.

    Induction Motor for Hybrid Electric Vehicles

    Electric or hybrid vehicles are not new today. I was working on this project in 2012 when the products were yet to launch in market. Although magnet-based motors are good from torque density perspective, SRM are good because no windings on stator, induction motor wins the race in terms of ruggedness, cost, and maturity of technology combined. As copper has higher conductivity than aluminum, use of copper-cage in induction machine rotor gives high torque. Our work in this domain is published here. I was working on two concepts there:
    1. Pole changing of induction machine
    2. Use of copper cage in place of aluminium cage

    Wireless charging for hybrid electric vehicles

    Wireless charging is a promising technology today. I was working on wireless charging pilot project with the following ratings.
    Power: 1 kW
    Distance: 30 cm
    Coil to coil power transfer efficiency: 95 %
    We worked on this project in collaboration with academia and implemented the project in a short span of 12 months from design to hardware testing. We used resonant converter topology for its simulation.

    High Frequency Model for Transformer Diagnostics

    I assessed transformer windings with the multiconductor transmission line approach to compute voltage distribution, if high frequency voltage impinges on the windings. Such voltage can lead to inter-turn insulation failure which is crucial and hence reduce life of power transformer. The work is published in IEEE Transactions on Power Delivery here.
